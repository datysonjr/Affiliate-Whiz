# RUNBOOK_SEO_SIGNAL_TRACKING.md

OpenClaw Affiliate Automation System

Purpose: Defines how to interpret early SEO signals BEFORE revenue appears.

Revenue is the LAST signal.
Ranking signals appear weeks earlier.

This runbook tells OpenClaw what to measure and how to interpret it.

---

## Signal Order of Appearance

SEO never jumps straight to money.

Signals appear in this order:

```
crawl → index → impressions → ranking movement → clicks → conversions
```

---

## PHASE 1 — CRAWL SIGNALS

Check:

- sitemap fetched regularly
- new URLs crawled quickly

If sitemap not fetched: **problem exists.**

---

## PHASE 2 — INDEX SIGNALS

Check:

- pages appear in `site:` search
- Search Console shows indexed count rising

Healthy site:

```
most pages indexed within 1 week
```

---

## PHASE 3 — IMPRESSION SIGNALS

**MOST IMPORTANT EARLY SIGNAL.**

Check:

- impressions rising weekly
- long-tail queries appearing

Even if clicks = zero:

**THIS IS GOOD.**

Impressions = Google testing your site.

---

## PHASE 4 — POSITION MOVEMENT

Check:

- pages entering positions 30–50
- then 20–30
- then top 20

Movement matters more than absolute position.

---

## PHASE 5 — EARLY CLICKS

Now traffic begins.

Small numbers normal.

Even:

```
5 clicks per day
```

means site is alive.

---

## Warning Signals

Watch for:

- impressions flatline after 3–4 weeks
- many pages never indexed
- pages suddenly deindexed
- ranking drops across entire site

These indicate structural issue.

---

## Healthy SEO Timeline

Typical pattern:

```
Week 1–2   → crawl/index
Week 2–4   → impressions begin
Week 4–8   → ranking movement
Week 6–12  → traffic growth
Week 8–16  → revenue signals
```

Any timeline faster is bonus.

---

## Final Rule

Do NOT judge a site by revenue early.

Judge by:

**crawl → index → impressions.**
